# OpenRelief Code of Conduct

## Purpose

OpenRelief serves people handling disaster recovery paperwork. Project spaces must stay respectful, safety-focused, and usable for contributors with different backgrounds.

## Expected Behavior

- Use clear, respectful language.
- Assume disaster survivors and case workers may read public issues.
- Keep feedback specific to code, docs, policy sources, tests, or safety risk.
- Protect privacy. Do not post real survivor PII, agency account details, or private documents.
- Flag safety concerns early, especially legal advice, eligibility promises, policy drift, or remote PII storage.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, slurs, or personal attacks.
- Posting private data without consent.
- Encouraging unsafe use of OpenRelief as legal advice, final eligibility review, or government submission service.
- Repeated off-topic disruption after maintainers ask for focus.
- Misrepresenting synthetic examples as real survivor cases.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns by opening a private security advisory when privacy or safety is involved, or by contacting project maintainers through repository owner channels. Include links, screenshots, timestamps, and short context when possible.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove comments, close discussions, restrict access, or block contributors when behavior creates safety, privacy, or participation risk. Enforcement should be proportional, documented, and focused on protecting project users and contributors.
